FTC Robotics – Team 28118 Robostallions

I began learning CAD in 8th grade using Onshape, diving into the world of 3D modeling and mechanical design. For the 2025 FTC season, I applied these skills to design and build the drivetrain for our robot for Team 28118, the Robostallions. This involved planning and assembling the core mechanical systems that allow the robot to move efficiently and reliably on the field.

During the project, I focused on precision and efficiency, making sure every gear, motor, and wheel was optimized for speed, torque, and durability. I collaborated closely with my teammates to integrate the drivetrain with other subsystems, such as the arm mechanism and intake system, ensuring the robot worked seamlessly as a whole.

I also learned problem-solving under constraints, adapting my designs when we faced challenges with weight distribution, space limitations, and motor placement. Using Onshape’s simulation and assembly tools, I was able to test movements virtually before building physical prototypes, saving time and materials.

This experience strengthened my CAD and engineering skills, taught me how to work effectively in a team environment, and gave me practical insight into robot design, prototyping, and iterative improvement. Beyond just building the robot, I gained experience in documenting designs, thinking critically about efficiency, and learning the importance of collaboration in STEM projects.

Overall, this FTC project was my first hands-on experience bringing a complex mechanical system from concept to reality, and it solidified my passion for robotics, design, and innovation. I’m excited to continue expanding my portfolio with new projects, pushing the limits of what I can design and create.
